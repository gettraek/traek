# Developer Spotlight: Interview Format

A recurring series featuring builders who use Træk. Published monthly. Format: async written interview — developer answers in their own words, lightly edited for clarity.

**Target length:** 600–900 words published
**Channel:** Blog (gettraek.com/blog), Dev.to, cross-posted to newsletter
**Cadence:** Monthly, timed to coincide with newsletter

---

## Sourcing Spotlights

- Scan GitHub for public projects using `@traek/*` packages
- Monitor Discord `#built-with-traek` channel
- Reach out to active playground users (opt-in via playground profile)
- Community nominations via `#nominate-a-builder` form
- Conference / meetup conversations

**Nomination form fields:**
- Builder name + GitHub/X handle
- Project name + description
- What problem Træk solved for them
- Link to project / demo
- Their email (for outreach)

## Interview Questions

Send these after they confirm. Let them skip any that don't fit.

### 1. Tell us about yourself and what you're building.

*What's your background? What does [project name] do, and who uses it?*

> [Keep it brief — 2–4 sentences. We want the person, not a PR blurb.]

---

### 2. What were you building before you discovered Træk, and what was broken about it?

*Walk us through what the previous version looked like — or what the alternative would have been.*

> [This is the most important answer. It frames the "before" and makes the contrast real. Encourage specificity: what did the workaround actually look like? What user feedback drove the change?]

---

### 3. How did you first hear about Træk, and what made you decide to try it?

*Was it a specific feature? A recommendation? A frustration with something else?*

> [Captures the discovery moment. Useful for understanding where our community learns about us.]

---

### 4. Walk us through how you integrated it.

*What adapter did you use? How long did the integration take? What surprised you — in a good or bad way?*

> [Technical, honest, specific. Don't soften the rough edges — they make the story credible.]

---

### 5. What did you build with it that you couldn't have shipped otherwise?

*Or: what would have taken significantly longer without Træk?*

> [This is the value prop moment. Get them to be concrete: a feature, a capability, a timeline.]

---

### 6. What's one thing you'd change about Træk?

*No library is perfect. We want to know.*

> [Shows we're not just collecting testimonials — we want real feedback. Also genuinely useful for the roadmap.]

---

### 7. What are you building next?

*Where is the project headed? Anything you're excited about?*

> [Gives readers a reason to follow the builder. Ends on forward momentum.]

---

### Optional: Share a code snippet you're proud of.

*Something from your Træk integration that you think is elegant, clever, or that others might find useful.*

> [Optional but highly encouraged. Code-forward audience. Even 5–10 lines is great.]

## Editorial Guidelines

**Editing principles:**
- Fix grammar and typos, preserve voice
- Do not rewrite sentences to sound "professional" — authenticity is the point
- Keep technical accuracy intact; don't simplify away the details
- Flag anything that seems like an error and ask before changing

**What to cut:**
- Marketing-speak ("robust", "seamless", "cutting-edge")
- Long preambles before the actual answer
- Anything that reads like a press release

**What to keep:**
- Opinions, even critical ones
- Jargon and technical specifics
- Humor and personality
- Rough edges in the experience

## Review & Approval Process

1. Draft sent to developer for review (3-day turnaround requested)
2. Developer approves or requests edits (up to 2 rounds)
3. Final approval before scheduling
4. Developer gets advance notice of publish date
5. Share the published post with the developer directly — ask them to share it

---

## Metrics to Track

- Published spotlights per quarter
- Average time from outreach to publish (target: < 3 weeks)
- Referral traffic from spotlight posts
- New GitHub stars / Discord joins correlated with publish dates
- Developers who contributed code or issues after being featured
